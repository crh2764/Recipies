        <div class="container" id="ingredients">

            <h3>Ingredients</h3>

            <p>1 package ramen noodles</p>

            <p>1 Tbsp parsley dried</p>

             <p>1/4th tsp garlic powder</p>

<p>1/4th tsp black pepper</p>

            <p>1/8th tsp table salt</p>

             <p>1/4th cup parmesan cheese (shelf stable)</p>

<p>3 Tbsp olive oil</p>

             <p>8 oz water</p>

 

        </div>

        <div class="container" id="backcountry-prep">

            <h3>Backcountry Prep:</h3>

            <p>1. Remove the Parmesan herb packet and add 8 oz of water</p>

            <p>2. Let stand to allow the noodles to rehydrate. This will take approximately 7 minutes if cooking the meal and 30 minutes if cold soaking. Avoid over-soaking noodles as they will become mushy. </p>

            <p>3. Consume or properly discard the noodle broth to practice the Leave No Trace principle. Add parmesan herb packet and 3 Tbsp (42 g) olive oil to the noodles. Stir to mix well and enjoy!

</p>

        </div>

    </div>

            <h3>Details</h3>

            <p>Prep Time: 5 min</p>

            <p>Cook Time: 25 min (longest)</p>

            <p>Cook Style: Cold soak </p>

        </div>

        <div class="container" id="home-prep">

            <h3>Home Prep:</h3>

            <p> Discard ramen noodle spice packet & put noodles in a bag or container to be used in the backcountry. </p>

            <p>Put the remaining dry ingredients in a separate bag to be stored inside the noodle bag or

container. </p>

            <p>. Pack 3 Tbsp (42 g) olive oil in a leakproof container to be added to the meal when consumed.We recommend double bagging the oil in the event there is a leak.</p>

        </div>

        <div class="container" id="prep-tips">

            <h3>Tips:</h3>

            <p>If preparing the meal for long-term storage, we recommend not adding the parmesan cheese until just before consuming the meal. By doing so, the shelf-life of the meal will be lengthened significantly.</p>
